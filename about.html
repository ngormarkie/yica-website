About us

Who We Are

A dynamic youth-led non-profit dedicated to addressing climate change and environmental issues in Sierra Leone.

We are the Youth Initiative for Climate Action - Sierra Leone (YICA-SL), a dynamic and youth-led non-profit organisation dedicated to addressing climate change and environmental issues in Sierra Leone. Founded in 2021, YICA-SL envisions a sustainable and resilient future where young people are actively engaged in climate action, environmental protection, and community development.

We are 100% youth-led, with all decision-makers under 35, working primarily with young people aged 15-35. We bridge the gap between community-level environmental action and national policy through education, advocacy, community action and policy engagement.

Our Mission

To empower and mobilise young people in Sierra Leone to become effective advocates, leaders, and changemakers in the fight against climate change and its impacts. Our work involves:

  • Raising awareness about climate change
  • Building the capacity of youth and women
  • Promoting community engagement in climate action
  • Advocating for youth involvement in policy processes
  • Showcasing youth-led initiatives

Our Vision

A sustainable and resilient Sierra Leone where young people lead climate action, environmental protection, and community development — ensuring that no community is left behind in the transition to a greener future.

Our Approach

We work at the intersection of grassroots action and national policy. Everything we do is designed to be youth-led, community-owned, and evidence-based:

  • Youth-led design: Young people lead the design, delivery and evaluation of every programme.
  • Community ownership: We work with communities to develop solutions rooted in local knowledge and priorities.
  • Evidence to action: Field assessments and consultations generate the data that drives our advocacy and programme design.
  • Policy engagement: We bring grassroots voices into national and international policy processes, including Sierra Leone's NDC review.
  • Partnerships: We collaborate with government, civil society, and international partners to amplify impact and reach.

Our Values

Youth Leadership

Young people are not just participants but leaders. We trust youth to design, deliver and evaluate climate solutions.

Inclusion

We prioritise the voices of young women, people with disabilities, and marginalised communities in all our work.

Accountability

We are transparent with our communities, partners and funders about how resources are used and what results are achieved.

Collaboration

Climate action requires collective effort. We build partnerships across sectors, borders and generations.
